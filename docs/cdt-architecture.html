Each modality is encoded independently into a 128-dim space; a late-fusion meta-learner compresses them into one CDT embedding — individual-discriminative, not just archetype-classifying.

Fusion internals

fusion/meta_learner.py · LateFusionMetaLearner
forward path · all four steps live inside LateFusionMetaLearner

Frozen per-modality embeddings are read from the cache. The learned MISSING vector replaces any structurally absent slot (the 750/1000 consumers without traces — distinct from training-time dropout), then the slots are concatenated into one [B, n×128] vector. A two-layer compressor (LayerNorm → GELU → dropout) squeezes the redundant cross-modal signal down to the 128-dim CDT embedding. A separate linear head emits the 7-class archetype logits, but only as a CE training proxy — it is discarded at inference. Individual identity is taught not by that head but by the NT-Xent term (right), which pulls two dropout views of the same consumer together.

All four boxes above are members of LateFusionMetaLearner: concat + MISSING are pre-MLP, fc1/fc2 are the compressor, classifier is the auxiliary CE head. forward_with_embedding() returns only the fc2 output (the CDT); the classifier is a training-time signal source, never shipped.

All modalities join on participant_id (e.g. price_lex_0042) — the individual consumer. The two newest modalities (bead 1it) also keep customer_id = PersonaConfig.persona_id (the archetype slug) for anonymous grouping; encoders and fusion group by participant_id, never customer_id. Before 1it, clickstream/campaign keyed only on the archetype id — collapsing every participant in an archetype to one record and making individual-level fusion impossible.

Application add-ons — real business scenarios

applications/ · proposed · none committed · see new-capabilities.md

The CDT answers "who is this person?". The capabilities below extend it toward "what will this person do?" — predicting choices, ranking products, simulating market response, flagging churn. Each is a thin trained head that consumes the frozen 128-dim CDT embedding; encoders and fusion are never modified. None are built yet — applications/ is created lazily by the first implementation bead.

P0Schema foundation
Product catalogue + a preference-driven choice model coupled to the acquisition trace. The critical path — gates M1 / L2 / M2. Today choices are rng.choice(alts), independent of behaviour.
high · critical pathschema+gen
L1Churn / defection
Churn head over the CDT, benchmarked against an RFM baseline. Viable without the product model — operates purely on the embedding. Scenario: retention / win-back targeting.

M1Choice prediction
Two-tower head — CDT tower × product tower → chosen-alternative probability. The primary product-level predictor. Needs P0. Scenario: "will they pick this?"
mediumembed
L2Personalised ranking
Thin wrapper over M1 — sorts a candidate product set by predicted choice probability. Scenario: recommendation / next-best-action.
low* · needs M1embed
M2Market demand sim
Aggregates M1 over a MarketState to simulate counterfactual demand curves. Validated against Option-B generator re-runs. Scenario: pricing / assortment what-if.
mediumedge
H1Temporal dynamics
Sequential CDT — lets the embedding evolve over time for drift detection and early-warning signals. No product model needed; the largest architecture change. Scenario: behaviour shift monitoring.

CACHEOne-shot export applications/_cache/cdt_embeddings.parquet — (participant_id, session_id → 128-float cdt). Every head reads the frozen CDT from this cache; none recompute it or retrain fusion.
Boundary invariant: applications/ imports only the frozen fusion meta-learner + EMBEDDING_DIM from schemas/, and may import Product / ChoiceSet / MarketState. It never touches encoder or fusion training code. Choices that need a product model (M1/L2/M2) are blocked until P0 lands; churn (L1) and temporal (H1) are viable on the CDT alone.
